Large Language Models: Architectural Evolution and Explainability

Samrat Kar - Dept of Computer Science, Amrita Visvavidyapeetham, Bangalore, India

Introduction

Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed AI capabilities, but their opaque, black-box nature poses significant challenges for trust, accountability, and safety in critical domains.

Research Focus: Exploring explainability mechanisms within LLM-based systems to improve transparency and understanding of AI-generated outputs.

Approach

  • Systematic literature review
  • Historical analysis of LLM architecture evolution
  • Exploration of explainable AI (XAI) techniques
  • Assessment of emerging trends and challenges

Key Architectural Innovations

Attention Mechanism

Introduced in "Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al., 2017), enabling models to focus on different parts of input sequences.

Efficient Transformers

  • Sparse Attention: Enables longer context handling
  • Mixture of Experts (MoE): Conditional compute for scaling
  • Switch Transformers: Scale to trillion parameters with efficient sparsity
  • Infini-Attention: Handling unlimited context length

Multimodal Integration

Explainable AI (XAI)

XAI refers to techniques that make the decision-making processes of AI systems transparent and interpretable to humans.

Why XAI for LLMs?
  • Enhance transparency and trust
  • Detect and address bias
  • Improve accountability
  • Strengthen security and robustness
  • Support ethical AI development

Challenges in XAI for LLMs

"Stop explaining black box machine learning models for high stakes decisions and use interpretable models instead." - Cynthia Rudin (2019)

Despite these challenges, XAI techniques continue to advance, with ongoing research focused on balancing model complexity with interpretability.

Conclusion

Key Findings

  • LLM architectures have evolved from simple rule-based systems to complex transformer-based models with billions or trillions of parameters
  • Transformer architecture with self-attention was the most significant breakthrough, enabling unprecedented scale and performance
  • Multimodal integration represents the next frontier, combining text with images, audio, and video
  • XAI is essential for responsible AI deployment, particularly in high-stakes domains
  • Explainability should be a core design principle, not an afterthought

Future Research Directions

While architectural advances in LLMs have unlocked extraordinary possibilities, the application of Explainable AI ensures these powerful tools are used safely, ethically, and equitably. By bridging the gap between complexity and comprehension, XAI empowers both creators and users of LLMs to navigate the challenges of this rapidly evolving landscape with confidence and clarity.
